# ── FAQs ─────────────────────────────────────
faqs:
  title: "Garlic Butter Chicken"
  items:
    - question: "Can I use chicken breasts instead of thighs?"
      answer: "Yes! Chicken breasts work fine but are less forgiving — they can dry out quickly. Pound them to an even thickness (about ¾ inch) and reduce the cook time to 4–5 minutes per side. Pull them off heat as soon as they hit 165°F (74°C)."
    - question: "Can I make this dairy-free?"
      answer: "You can substitute the heavy cream with full-fat coconut cream and use a dairy-free butter alternative. The sauce will have a very slight coconut undertone but it's still delicious. Skip the parmesan or use a dairy-free alternative."
    - question: "What do I serve this with?"
      answer: "This is incredible over white rice, pasta (linguine or fettuccine), mashed potatoes, or cauliflower mash for a low-carb option. Crusty bread to soak up the sauce is also highly recommended."
    - question: "Can I make this ahead of time?"
      answer: "The chicken is best fresh, but you can make the sauce ahead and store it separately. Reheat the sauce gently on low heat, add a splash of chicken broth to loosen it, then warm the chicken in the sauce before serving."
    - question: "Why did my cream sauce break or look grainy?"
      answer: "This happens when cream is added to a pan that's too hot or boiled too aggressively. Always reduce heat to medium-low before adding cream, and stir constantly as you pour it in. If it does break, remove from heat and whisk in a tablespoon of cold butter — this usually rescues it."

This **Creamy Garlic Butter Chicken** is the kind of recipe that makes everyone think you spent hours in the kitchen — but the secret is it comes together in just **30 minutes** in a single pan. Juicy, golden-seared chicken thighs sit in a velvety, rich garlic cream sauce that is absolutely irresistible spooned over rice or soaked up with crusty bread.

If you love easy weeknight dinners that feel special, this one is going straight into your regular rotation.

## Why You'll Love This Recipe

- **One pan, 30 minutes:** Everything from sear to sauce happens in the same skillet — minimal cleanup.
- **Restaurant-quality at home:** The garlic butter cream sauce tastes like something you'd order at a nice Italian restaurant.
- **Crispy skin, juicy inside:** The dry-sear technique gives you a deeply golden crust without drying out the meat.
- **Naturally gluten-free:** No flour, no roux — the sauce thickens naturally from the cream and parmesan.
- **Endlessly versatile:** Serve over rice, pasta, mashed potatoes, or cauliflower for a low-carb option.
- **Crowd-pleasing:** Kids and adults both go crazy for this one.

## Key Ingredients

[KEY_INGREDIENTS_IMAGE: /images/recipes/garlic-butter-chicken/ingredients.jpg]

- **Chicken thighs.** Bone-in, skin-on thighs are the move here — they stay incredibly juicy and give you that crispy golden skin. Boneless thighs work too if that's what you have.
- **Butter.** We're using 3 tablespoons total: one for searing, two for building the sauce. Use unsalted so you control the salt level.
- **Fresh garlic.** Six cloves minimum. Fresh garlic is non-negotiable for this recipe — jarred minced garlic just won't give you the same depth of flavour.
- **Heavy cream.** This is what makes the sauce rich and velvety. Don't substitute with half-and-half or milk — the sauce won't thicken properly.
- **Parmesan cheese.** Freshly grated from a block melts into the sauce smoothly. Pre-shredded parmesan has anti-caking agents that can make sauces grainy.
- **Chicken broth.** A splash of broth deglazes the pan and lifts all those golden browned bits — that's where all the flavour lives.

## Easy Substitutions & Variations

- **Chicken breasts:** Can be used instead of thighs — pound to even thickness and reduce cook time. Watch carefully as they overcook faster.
- **Add sun-dried tomatoes:** Stir in 2–3 tablespoons of chopped sun-dried tomatoes with the garlic for a Tuscan-style twist.
- **Add spinach:** Toss in 2 large handfuls of baby spinach right at the end and stir until just wilted.
- **Make it spicy:** Add ½ teaspoon of red pepper flakes with the garlic.
- **Dairy-free version:** Use full-fat coconut cream instead of heavy cream and dairy-free butter. Skip parmesan or use a plant-based alternative.
- **Add mushrooms:** Sauté 8 oz of sliced cremini mushrooms after removing the chicken, before adding garlic.

## How to Store

**Refrigerator:** Store chicken and sauce together in an airtight container for up to **3 days**. The sauce will thicken considerably when cold — this is normal.

**Reheating:** Warm gently in a skillet over low heat with 2–3 tablespoons of chicken broth stirred in to loosen the sauce back to its original creamy consistency. Avoid microwave if possible, as it can make the chicken tough and cause the sauce to separate.

**Freezing:** Not recommended for this recipe. Cream-based sauces tend to separate and become grainy when frozen and thawed, which ruins the texture.
